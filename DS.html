Seoul-Sky Restaurant



This rare revolving restaurant in the Philippines offers a 360-degree view of the Metro Manila skyline.

Best of all, it’s easily accessible from key points in the National Capital Region.




Enjoy Korean food and the Metro Manila skyline while you dine at this revolving restaurant.




Yes, it slowly spins, so you get a 360-degree view of the city while enjoying modum-jeon, bibimbap, and more.

Intramuros



Intramuros may be four centuries-old, but the walled city still manages to surprise even those who have been there a couple of times.




From its culturally-important heritage structures to new attractions that make even the youth interested in its old charm,




there are many ways to spend a day in the historic fort. Other than history, Intramuros has plenty to offer:

traditional Filipino arts, alternative mobility, and even a shot from the oldest distillery in the country
